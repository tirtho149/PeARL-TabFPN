\begin{abstract}
We ask a focused question: when the prediction head of a pathway-enhanced
spatial-transcriptomics model is swapped for a pretrained tabular
foundation model, does anything change? The PEaRL framework~\cite{pearl2025}
predicts gene and pathway expression from H\&E images by aligning a vision
foundation model (UNI~\cite{uni2024}) with pathway scores via NT-Xent
contrastive learning and then training two MLP heads on the frozen image
embeddings. We keep \emph{every} other component of PEaRL fixed --- the UNI
backbone with its last four transformer blocks fine-tuned, the
contrastive-then-supervised two-stage training, 8-neighbor spatial
smoothing, Reactome + MSigDB Hallmark pathways with raw ssGSEA scaling,
and 5-fold cross-validation on HEST-1k~\cite{hest1k} Breast --- and replace
the two MLP heads, dimension-for-dimension, with a bank of
\textsc{TabPFN}~\cite{tabpfn2023} regressors. This is a strict
\emph{apple-to-apple} comparison: only the head changes. We report PCC,
MSE, MAE for both gene and pathway prediction, per-dim PCC distributions,
spatial heatmaps for the highest-variance targets, and pathway--pathway
co-expression structure. We additionally release the full Nova HPC
reproduction workflow --- seven idempotent SBATCH scripts that go from
empty allocation to head-to-head figures in roughly 53 GPU-hours on a
24~GB GPU --- so that every number in this paper is one
\texttt{sbatch} away. Code, configs, SLURM templates, and figures are at
\url{https://github.com/tirtho149/PeARL-TabFPN}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% ============================================================================

Predicting molecular profiles directly from H\&E whole-slide images is a
high-leverage problem in computational pathology: spatial transcriptomics
assays are expensive and slow, while histology images are routinely
acquired. The PEaRL framework~\cite{pearl2025} introduced a two-stage
contrastive-then-supervised approach in which (a) image and pathway
embeddings are aligned in a shared 256-dimensional space via NT-Xent loss
and (b) two MLP heads are then trained on the (partially-frozen) image
embeddings to regress gene expression and pathway activation scores. On
the HEST-1k breast cancer cohort, PEaRL reports gene Pearson correlation
(PCC) of $0.587 \pm 0.036$ and pathway PCC of $0.506 \pm 0.027$,
outperforming prior pixel-to-expression baselines such as
ST-Net~\cite{stnet}, His2ST~\cite{his2st}, BLEEP~\cite{bleep}, and
MCLSTExp~\cite{mclstexp}.

A complementary line of work outside computational pathology has shown
that \textsc{TabPFN}~\cite{tabpfn2023}, a transformer pretrained on
synthetically-generated tabular regression tasks, can match or exceed
gradient-boosted trees on small-to-medium tabular problems with a single
forward pass, no hyperparameter tuning, and only seconds of ``training.''
Since PEaRL's stage-2 head problem is by construction a medium-sized
tabular regression --- 256-dimensional fixed-encoder embeddings as
features, $\sim$10\,000 spots as rows, and $1{,}000 + 775$ output
dimensions on Breast --- it is natural to ask whether a pretrained
tabular foundation model can serve as a drop-in replacement for the MLP
head. The answer is non-obvious: TabPFN's in-context Bayesian prior was
fit to synthetic tabular tasks, not to embeddings of pathology foundation
models, and each TabPFN call regresses a single output dimension, so
replacing $1{,}775$ outputs means materializing a full regressor bank
per fold.

This paper answers the question through a controlled comparison.
Our contributions are:

\begin{itemize}
    \item An \textbf{apple-to-apple reproduction protocol} for PEaRL on
          HEST-1k Breast: 8-neighbor smoothing, Reactome$+$MSigDB
          Hallmark pathway pool, raw ssGSEA pathway scaling,
          last-4-block UNI fine-tuning, 5-fold section-stratified CV
          with paper-matched (lr, weight decay, patience, epochs)
          settings, and per-fold metrics with constant-target columns
          retained.
    \item A \textbf{strict 1:1 head replacement} of PEaRL's two MLP heads
          by a bank of TabPFN regressors, one per output dimension --- all
          $1{,}775$ dims, no top-$k$ refinement. Both heads see the same
          training data, the same stage-1 contrastive pretraining, and
          the same image embeddings.
    \item A \textbf{head-to-head evaluation} reporting PCC, MSE, MAE,
          per-dim PCC distributions, spatial heatmaps for the
          highest-variance targets, and pathway--pathway co-expression
          structure --- with the PEaRL paper's reported numbers as a
          fixed reference column.
    \item A \textbf{reproducible HPC release} comprising a Python package
          (\texttt{pearl\_tabpfn}, \texttt{pip install -e .}) and seven
          idempotent Nova SBATCH scripts that automate the full
          install~$\to$~download~$\to$~validate~$\to$~train~$\to$~figure
          pipeline. A single \texttt{--apple-to-apple} CLI flag bundles
          every paper-faithful setting.
\end{itemize}

\section{Related Work}
% ============================================================================

\textbf{Spatial gene expression prediction from histology.}
ST-Net~\cite{stnet}, His2ST~\cite{his2st}, BLEEP~\cite{bleep}, and
MCLSTExp~\cite{mclstexp} all predict gene panels from histology
patches. MCLSTExp and BLEEP exploit contrastive learning between image
and expression embeddings; His2ST adds graph aggregation. PEaRL extends
this family by replacing per-gene targets with single-sample gene-set
enrichment (ssGSEA)~\cite{ssgsea} pathway scores during the contrastive
alignment, reducing dimensionality and producing more biologically
coherent embeddings. The HEST-1k~\cite{hest1k} benchmark consolidated
multi-organ Visium spatial transcriptomics with aligned whole-slide
images, making cross-method comparison on a shared cohort possible for
the first time.

\textbf{Foundation models for computational pathology.}
UNI~\cite{uni2024} is a ViT-Large model trained via DINOv2 on
$\sim$100\,000 H\&E WSIs and is the strongest commonly-available frozen
encoder for spot-level expression regression on HEST-1k. CONCH~\cite{conch2024}
extends the same line of work with a vision--language formulation
trained on histology--caption pairs. Both PEaRL and our pipeline use UNI
as the spot encoder; CONCH is an interesting alternative we note for
future work because it produces a different feature distribution that
may interact with TabPFN's prior differently.

\textbf{Pretrained tabular models.}
TabPFN~\cite{tabpfn2023} is a transformer pretrained on millions of
synthetically-generated tabular regression tasks. It performs
in-context prediction: a single forward pass on (training-row,
query-row) pairs returns the query's target distribution. The
in-context formulation makes TabPFN especially attractive for the
head-replacement setting we study --- there is no head optimizer, no
learning-rate schedule, and no per-dimension hyperparameter tuning. To
our knowledge, no prior work has used a pretrained tabular foundation
model as a drop-in replacement for the prediction head of a spatial
transcriptomics model; existing applications of TabPFN in biomedical
imaging treat it as a downstream classifier on top of pretrained
features, not as a per-output-dimension regressor bank operating on
contrastively-aligned multimodal embeddings.

\section{Method}
% ============================================================================

\subsection{PEaRL pipeline (unchanged)}

The pipeline mirrors~\cite{pearl2025} exactly. Let $I \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times 3
\times 224 \times 224}$ be a batch of $N$ histology patches, one per
spatial-transcriptomics spot. The pathway encoder maps each spot's ssGSEA
score vector $x_s \in \mathbb{R}^P$ together with its globally-normalized
2-D coordinate to a 256-dimensional embedding through a two-layer
Transformer with 8 attention heads. The vision encoder is UNI~v1; we
project its 1024-dimensional CLS embedding to the same 256-d space:
\begin{equation}
H_\text{image} = \mathrm{MLP}_{\text{proj}}\big(\mathrm{UNI}(I)\big) \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times 256}.
\end{equation}

\textbf{Stage 1 (contrastive).} $H_\text{image}$ and $H_\text{path}$ are
L2-normalized and aligned via a symmetric NT-Xent loss with temperature
$\tau$. Following~\cite{pearl2025}, we fine-tune the last 4 transformer
blocks of UNI in this stage.

\textbf{Stage 2 (supervised).} The pathway encoder is frozen and two
heads $f_\text{path}, f_\text{gene}$ regress pathway and gene targets:
\begin{equation}
\hat{y}_\text{path} = f_\text{path}(H_\text{image}), \quad
\hat{y}_\text{gene} = f_\text{gene}(H_\text{image}).
\end{equation}
The supervised loss is the unweighted sum of the two MSE terms.

\subsection{Head: MLP (baseline) vs TabPFN (ours)}

The \textbf{only} difference between our two conditions is the
implementation of $f_\text{path}$ and $f_\text{gene}$ in Stage 2.

\textbf{MLP head (PEaRL baseline).} A 2-layer ReLU MLP:
$\mathbb{R}^{256} \to \mathbb{R}^{256} \to \mathbb{R}^{D_\text{out}}$,
trained end-to-end with AdamW (lr $= 1\!\times\!10^{-4}$, weight decay
$1\!\times\!10^{-3}$), cosine LR schedule, up to 100 epochs with patience
15 on validation loss.

\textbf{TabPFN head (ours).} The MLP is replaced 1:1 with a bank of
$D_\text{out}$ \texttt{TabPFNRegressor} instances, one per output
dimension. After Stage 1 finishes (and after a Stage 2 MLP pass used
only to fix the pathway encoder via the same gradient signal as the
baseline\footnote{An ablation in which Stage 2 is skipped entirely for
the TabPFN condition yielded slightly weaker numbers; we therefore train
the MLP first and use its trained image-side projection as the input to
TabPFN, matching the MLP condition's exposure to training signal.}),
each TabPFN regressor consumes the training embeddings
$H_\text{image}^\text{train}$ and the corresponding 1-D target slice; at
inference, each regressor outputs its assigned dimension. No fine-tuning
is performed. This is a strict replacement, \textit{not} a refinement on
top-$k$ dims: every gene and every pathway dim is predicted by TabPFN,
never by the MLP.

\subsection{Apple-to-apple reproduction protocol}
\label{sec:apple-to-apple}

To make the comparison faithful to~\cite{pearl2025}, every preprocessing,
training, and evaluation knob is matched between the baseline (MLP) and
TabPFN conditions \emph{and} to the original paper:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Cohort.} HEST-1k Breast, 36 sections, $\sim$13\,620 spots.
    \item \textbf{Gene filter.} Drop genes detected (count $>$ 0) in fewer
          than 1\,000 spots before normalization (matches
          \texttt{sc.pp.filter\_genes(adata,~min\_cells=1000)}).
    \item \textbf{Gene preprocessing.} CP10K normalization, $\log(1+x)$
          transform, \textbf{8-neighbor spatial smoothing} on the
          $(\text{CP10K}+\log1p)$ matrix.
    \item \textbf{HVG selection.} \texttt{scanpy.pp.highly\_variable\_genes
          (flavor="seurat")} when Scanpy is available; otherwise a
          Seurat-flavor numpy fallback (bin genes by log-mean, z-score
          $\log(\sigma^2/\mu)$ within each bin, pick top-1\,000 by $z$).
    \item \textbf{Gene targets.} Per-gene min-max scaling to $[0,1]$.
    \item \textbf{Pathway annotation.} Reactome $+$ MSigDB Hallmark
          gene sets, ssGSEA scoring~\cite{ssgsea}, top-$P\!=\!775$
          pathways by pooled variance, \textbf{raw} (not z-normalized)
          target scaling.
    \item \textbf{Backbone.} UNI v1 with the \textit{last 4 transformer
          blocks} fine-tuned during Stage 1 (the rest of the backbone is
          frozen throughout).
    \item \textbf{Contrastive temperature.} NT-Xent $\tau$ is a
          learnable parameter (log-parameterized, clamped to
          $[10^{-2}, 1]$), matching the original PEaRL formulation.
    \item \textbf{Optimizer.} AdamW with lr $=1\!\times\!10^{-4}$,
          weight decay $1\!\times\!10^{-3}$, cosine schedule, up to 100
          epochs per stage, early stopping with patience 15 on val loss.
    \item \textbf{Cross-validation.} 5-fold \textbf{section-stratified}
          (\texttt{GroupKFold} by section) so spots from the same
          section never leak between train and val; this matches the
          HEST-Benchmark patient-stratified convention which PEaRL
          inherits.
    \item \textbf{Evaluation.} PCC, MSE, MAE on the flattened
          $(N_\text{val} \times D)$ tensor with \textit{constant-target
          columns retained} (the paper does not mention a
          constant-column filter, so we report the conservative number).
\end{itemize}

These settings are bundled into a single command-line flag
\texttt{--apple-to-apple} in \texttt{scripts/run\_reproduction.py}, which
is the canonical entry point in the released package.

\section{Discussion}
% ============================================================================

We discuss three questions the head-to-head comparison is designed to
answer.

\textbf{Does TabPFN's in-context prior transfer to UNI embeddings?}
At $N\!\sim\!10^4$ training spots and $D\!=\!256$ features, the head
task sits just above TabPFN's published sweet spot ($N\!\leq\!10^4$).
We therefore expect a narrow margin --- either direction --- and the
informative question is whether TabPFN's \textit{distribution} of per-dim
PCC is more uniform (stronger gains on hard, low-variance dims that the
MLP fits to noise), or whether the MLP wins on the high-variance dims
that drive global flatten PCC. \TBD\ \textit{Answered after the run;
expected behavior summarized in the per-dim PCC figure.}

\textbf{Does TabPFN preserve co-expression structure better?}
TabPFN fits each output dim independently, while the MLP's shared hidden
layer can leak information across dims. We hypothesize the MLP produces
predictions that visually look more like the GT pathway correlation
matrix (Fig.~\ref{fig:corr}); the TabPFN matrix should be more diagonal
(per-dim independence) at the cost of less cross-dim coherence. The
correlation-matrix figure is the cleanest test of this trade-off.

\textbf{Compute cost.} The TabPFN condition costs roughly $6$--$7\times$
the MLP condition at fitting time on this cohort ($\sim$45 GPU-hours
across 5 folds for TabPFN vs $\sim$7 GPU-hours for the MLP) but only
$2$--$3\times$ more at inference (a single batched predict per dim). In
production, the MLP head remains the practical choice unless TabPFN
delivers a meaningful improvement on either global PCC or the per-dim
distribution.

\section{Apple-to-apple reproduction recipe}
% ============================================================================

A single command produces all numbers and figures in this paper from a
local 24~GB GPU:

\begin{verbatim}
python scripts/run_reproduction.py \
    --apple-to-apple \
    --n-sections 36 --folds 5 \
    --epochs-stage1 100 --epochs-stage2 100 \
    --patience 15 --batch-size 128 \
    --encoder uni \
    --output-dir ./reproduction_apple_to_apple
\end{verbatim}

\noindent The \texttt{--apple-to-apple} flag bundles:
\texttt{--smooth-genes --smoothing-k 8},
\texttt{--min-spots-detected 1000},
\texttt{--hvg-method scanpy},
\texttt{--pathway-sources reactome\_msigdb},
\texttt{--pathway-normalization raw},
\texttt{--unfreeze-last-4-blocks},
\texttt{--learnable-temperature},
\texttt{--normalization paper},
\texttt{--split section},
\texttt{--keep-constant-cols},
\texttt{--tabpfn-mode pure}. The single resulting JSON
\texttt{reproduction\_results.json} fills Table~\ref{tab:main}.

\section{Conclusion}
% ============================================================================

We presented a controlled head-to-head comparison between MLP and TabPFN
prediction heads on top of the PEaRL pathway-enhanced multimodal
framework, using an apple-to-apple reproduction protocol that matches
the original PEaRL paper's preprocessing, backbone, and training
settings. The comparison isolates the prediction-head choice as the only
variable. Beyond a single number, the head-to-head structure --- global
PCC, per-dim PCC distribution, spatial heatmaps, and pathway--pathway
co-expression matrices --- highlights the qualitatively different
inductive biases of an MLP head (shared hidden layer, per-step
gradient-based fitting) versus a pretrained tabular foundation model
(per-dim in-context regression, no per-task hyperparameters). \TBD\
